About Pickleball

History & Origin

Pickleball was invented in 1965 on Bainbridge Island, a short ferry ride from Seattle, Washington. Three dads – Joel Pritchard, Bill Bell, and Barney McCallum – created the game for their families who were bored during summer vacation.

The original purpose was to provide a game that the whole family could play together. The Pritchard property had an old badminton court, but they couldn't find enough rackets for everyone to play. They improvised with ping-pong paddles and a perforated plastic ball.

How the Game Got Its Name

There are two stories about how pickleball got its name:

  1. The Boat Theory: According to Joel Pritchard's wife Joan, she started calling the game pickleball because "the combination of different sports reminded me of the pickle boat in crew where oarsmen were chosen from the leftovers of other boats."
  2. The Dog Theory: The Pritchards had a dog named Pickles who would chase after the ball and run off with it. Hence, "Pickles' ball" which was later shortened to "pickleball."

Growth & Popularity

From its humble beginnings, pickleball has grown to become one of the fastest-growing sports in America. According to the Sports & Fitness Industry Association (SFIA), pickleball participation grew by 39.3% from 2019 to 2021.

Today, the sport is played in thousands of locations across the United States and is gaining popularity internationally as well. The USA Pickleball Association (USAPA) was formed in 1984 to promote the growth and development of the sport.

What is Pickleball?

Pickleball is a paddle sport that combines elements of badminton, tennis, and table tennis. It's played both indoors and outdoors on a badminton-sized court with a slightly modified tennis net.

Players use solid paddles made of wood or composite materials to hit a perforated polymer ball over the net.

Pickleball Facts

  • Pickleball is played in all 50 states
  • There are over 8,000 locations to play in the US
  • The average age of players is decreasing as younger generations discover the sport
  • A pickleball court is 20' x 44', smaller than a tennis court
  • The game can be played as singles or doubles
  • Professional pickleball tournaments now offer substantial prize money
