Court Types

Pickleball courts come in various types, each with its own advantages and considerations:

Court Type Advantages Considerations
Dedicated Outdoor Proper dimensions, permanent nets, often have lights Weather dependent, may have wait times at popular locations
Converted Tennis Courts Widely available, proper surface May need to bring portable nets, temporary lines
Indoor Courts Climate controlled, consistent playing conditions Usually require fees, may need reservations
Multi-use Gym Floors Often available at community centers, good for beginners May have limited hours, shared with other activities

Court Etiquette

When playing at public courts, remember these etiquette guidelines:

  • Respect court time limits, especially when others are waiting
  • Clean up after yourself and dispose of trash properly
  • Be mindful of noise levels, particularly in residential areas
  • Follow local rules regarding reservations and court usage
  • Be welcoming to new players and willing to rotate in players when appropriate

Starting Your Own Court

Interested in building a court at your home or community?

Basic requirements:

  • 44' x 20' flat surface (concrete, asphalt, or modular sport tiles)
  • Proper drainage
  • Permanent or portable net system
  • Court lines (painted or taped)
