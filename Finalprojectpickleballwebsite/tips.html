Pickleball Strategies & Tips

Essential Strategies for All Skill Levels

Whether you're a beginner or an advanced player, these strategies will help you improve your game and win more matches.

Beginner Tips

  1. Master the Serve: Focus on consistency rather than power. A good serve is one that lands deep in the opponent's service court.
  2. Remember the Two-Bounce Rule: The serve must bounce, and the return of serve must bounce before either team can volley the ball.
  3. Get to the Non-Volley Zone Line: After the two bounces, both teams should move forward to the non-volley zone line (kitchen line) for the best court position.
  4. Keep the Ball in Play: Consistency is key. Focus on returning the ball rather than trying to hit winners.
  5. Watch the Ball: Keep your eye on the ball all the way to your paddle. This improves accuracy and timing.

Intermediate Strategies

  1. The Third Shot Drop: After serving and receiving the return, hit a soft shot that lands in the opponent's non-volley zone. This prevents them from attacking and gives your team time to move forward.
  2. Dinking Game: Practice soft shots that just clear the net and land in the opponent's non-volley zone. This creates opportunities for your opponent to make errors.
  3. Court Positioning: In doubles, partners should move as a unit, staying side by side and adjusting positions based on where the ball is hit.
  4. Shot Selection: Learn when to hit soft shots (dinks) versus when to attack with power. Generally, hit down on balls above net height and lift balls that are below net height.
  5. Communication: Call out "mine" or "yours" to avoid confusion with your partner. Also communicate strategy between points.

Improve Your Game

Consistent practice, proper technique, and smart strategy are the keys to improving your pickleball game. Focus on mastering the fundamentals before moving on to advanced techniques.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Hitting the ball too hard when a soft shot would be more effective
  • Standing too far back from the non-volley zone line
  • Not communicating with your partner in doubles
  • Hitting down on balls below net height
  • Trying to hit winners from difficult positions
  • Failing to watch the ball all the way to your paddle
  • Hitting toward the stronger player in doubles
