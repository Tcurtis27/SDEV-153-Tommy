Rules & How to Play

Diagram of a standard pickleball court showing dimensions and zones

Basic Rules of Pickleball

Pickleball is played on a badminton-sized court (20' x 44') with a modified tennis net. The game can be played as singles or doubles, with doubles being the most common format.

The Court

The pickleball court is divided into sections:

  • Non-Volley Zone (Kitchen): A 7-foot area on each side of the net where players cannot volley the ball (hit it in the air without letting it bounce).
  • Right/Even Service Court: The area to the right of the center line, as you face the net.
  • Left/Odd Service Court: The area to the left of the center line, as you face the net.
  • Baseline: The back line of the court.

Serving Rules

The serve must be made underhand and below waist level:

  1. The server must keep one foot behind the baseline during the serve.
  2. The serve is made diagonally to the opponent's service court.
  3. Only one serve attempt is allowed (except if the ball touches the net and lands in the correct service court, which is a "let" and is reserved).
  4. The serving team can only score points.
  5. In doubles, both players on a team serve before the serve passes to the opponents (except for the first service sequence of each game).

The Two-Bounce Rule

After the serve:

  1. The receiving team must let the ball bounce once before returning it.
  2. The serving team must then let the return bounce once before hitting it.
  3. After these two bounces, the ball can be volleyed or played off the bounce.

Scoring

Games are typically played to 11 points, and a team must win by 2 points.

In doubles, the score is called out as three numbers:

  1. The serving team's score
  2. The receiving team's score
  3. The server number (1 or 2, indicating which player on the team is serving)

Faults

A fault occurs when:

  • The ball is hit out of bounds
  • The ball does not clear the net
  • The ball is volleyed in the non-volley zone (kitchen)
  • The ball is volleyed before the two-bounce rule is satisfied
  • A player touches the net or the non-volley zone while volleying the ball

Basic Rules

Pickleball combines elements of tennis, badminton, and ping-pong. The rules are simple and the game is easy to learn, but can develop into a fast-paced, competitive game for experienced players.

Pickleball Terminology

Kitchen
The non-volley zone that extends 7 feet from the net on each side.
Dink
A soft shot hit from the non-volley zone that arcs over the net and lands in the opponent's non-volley zone.
Erne
A volley shot hit near the net post while standing outside the non-volley zone.
Poach
When a player crosses over to their partner's side to hit the ball.
Stacking
A strategy where players position themselves to maintain their preferred sides of the court.
